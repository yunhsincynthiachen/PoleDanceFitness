Mika: “POLE DANCING??!???!”

I was on the phone with my dad after one of our first classes, and feeling really excited about how good and strong it had made me feel. I have a very open relationship with my parents and decided that this was a good time to let them know what I was up to.

“So there’s this thing I’ve been doing that you’re going to disapprove of.”

“Haha okay, what is it?” (I guess this didn’t seem out of the ordinary for me.)

“I’m taking pole dancing lessons.”

“POLE DANCING??!???!”

“Yupp.”

“Okay…"

He paused for a while. This was clearly an awkward conversation for him to have with his daughter, and I think he was taking a moment to be open-minded about it.

"… Um… okay…. Okay. Okay. Sorry, I’m really at a loss for words.”

After we shrugged that news off and finished talking, he handed the phone to my mom.

“Dad said you have something to tell me?”

“Yeah. I’m doing a Passionate Pursuit in pole dancing. We’re looking into the fitness and stigma surrounding the dance…”

“What?? Wait, really? No. No no no no no no no. What? No. No. Really??”

After the shock died down a little bit, I clarified to her that the classes we were taking were exercise classes; we were not learning how to strip. This was a bit of a relief for her, or would at least help her get through this.
